Here are some common Frisbee violations and what they mean:



A player commits a foul if they get in a collision with someone else that caused them to drop the disc. Fouls can also be called when a player injures another player

A thrower can call a strip if the disc got knocked out of the thrower's hand when they didn't mean to throw it

A player can commit a travel if they take unnessesary steps while holding the disc. A few steps after catching the disc are generally allowed, but they have to come to a full stop immediately after catching the disc

You can call a walk-in if a player caught the disc then walked into the end zone. You can also call a walk-out if you caught the disc inbounds but then walked out of bounds

A defender can call a stall if the person throwing the disc takes too long to throw the disc. In most regulation games, a player has 10 seconds to throw the disc before their defender can call a stall
